Google strives to cultivate a wholly inclusive workplace around the globe. A key component of that vision is empowering women to pursue their dreams and build tools that change the world. We believe that when organizations create the right environments, programs and policies, women in technology can thrive.

That’s why, in conjunction with International Women’s Day on March 8th, Google is partnering with Women Techmakers to launch a series of global events geared towards increasing visibility, community, and resources for technical women in our industry. The events will feature panel discussions with female technology leaders, hands-on career-planning workshops, networking opportunities, and more.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is International Women’s Day?

International Women’s Day (IWD) is marked on March 8th every year. In different regions, the focus of celebrations ranges from general respect and appreciation toward women to a celebration of women’s economic, political, and social achievements.

Why does Google support International Women’s Day (IWD)?

From Ada Lovelace to Grace Hopper, we’re proud of the contributions women have made to the field of computer science. And we’re excited by the contributions women continue to make today. International Women’s Day is an opportunity to celebrate women in tech.

What is Women Techmakers?

Women Techmakers is a Google-led initiative that was founded within Google’s Developer Relations team and spearheaded by Megan Smith, VP of Google[X]. To date, Women Techmakers has been a series of Google Developers Live shows, as well as talks and networking events during Google I/O. In 2014, Women Techmakers is expanding into a global program that will increase the visibility, community, and resources for technical women in industry.

What are Google’s plans for the Women Techmakers celebration of International Women’s Day?

In conjunction with International Women’s Day on March 8th, Google is launching a series of global events geared towards increasing visibility, community, and resources for technical women in our industry. The events will feature panel discussions with female technology leaders, hands-on career-planning workshops, networking opportunities, and more. When will these events take place?

There are four application-based Summits taking place at the Google office in Mountain View, New York, Campus London and Campus Tel Aviv on Saturday, March 8th. Additional events range between between March 1st - March 22nd. For more information on an event near you, click here.

I am interested in participating in one of the celebrations, but I am not female. Can I still attend?

Absolutely! We would love to have the support and perspective of all genders for this initiative. Google believes in inclusivity both in our workplace and for all of our programs and events.

Will childcare be provided?

Childcare will be provided at the International Women’s Day Summits in Mountain View and New York City. If you are in need of these services, please indicate so in your application. Unfortunately, we are unable to provide childcare for other event locations at this time.

Who do I reach out to with questions?

For logistical event questions, please email our alias: IWD@google.com and include the location of the event you’d like more information about. All other inquiries should be directed to press@google.com.
